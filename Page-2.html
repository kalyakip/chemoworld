eyes in the ocean

Oceans make up 70% of the Earth’s surface, yet we know very little about them. They are home to millions of species, many of which are still undiscovered. They regulate the climate, provide food and resources, and support human well-being. They are also facing multiple threats, such as overfishing, pollution, acidification, and warming. To protect and conserve the oceans, we need to explore and understand them better. 

About seamore

Seamore is a project that aims to address the urgent need for ocean exploration and conservation, as climate change is rapidly altering marine ecosystems and the behavior of their inhabitants. Seamore will help in monitoring marine life, tracking specific species (especially endangered ones), and assessing the health of ecosystems such as seagrass and coral reefs. The data that seamore collects will be stored on an open server where anyone can access it (like a gallery) and get to appreciate the ocean. In the future, we aim to use seamore to aid in restoration activities such as coral reef restoration.

The Challenge

As climate change disrupts the world's oceans, the behavior of marine life is evolving in response to these shifting conditions.

our current methods of exploration are struggling to keep up with these changes, leaving us in the dark about the lives and behaviors of marine species.

Current methods of ocean exploration, often reliant on robots or intrepid divers, have brought us a wealth of data. However, this deluge of information has led to a pressing problem—an overwhelming backlog of unprocessed data.

Sustainability

Marine Biodiversity and Ecosystem Conservation.

Species Identification: Seamore can identify and document various marine species, including endangered ones, which is crucial for monitoring and conserving biodiversity.

Data Accessibility: Seamore stores the data it collects on an open server, making it accessible to researchers, conservationists, and the public. This promotes transparency and collaborative efforts to protect marine biodiversity.

Ecosystem Monitoring: Seamore can track the health of ecosystems such as seagrass beds and coral reefs, providing valuable data for the conservation and restoration of these vital habitats.

Behavioral Changes: Seamore helps researchers understand how climate change is affecting the behavior of marine life, contributing to our knowledge of how ecosystems are adapting to environmental shifts.

A Vision for Tomorrow

In the future, Seamore envisions itself as a pioneer in ocean restoration and conservation, utilizing cutting-edge technology to revitalize marine ecosystems. Seamore plants coral reefs and seagrass beds with precision, breathing life into once-barren seabeds. Additionally, it combats marine pollution, purifying the oceans and fostering a harmonious coexistence between humans and nature. Beyond technology, Seamore's mission serves as a beacon of hope, inspiring future generations to protect marine life.
